Mindset & Culture

Psychological Safety

Share openly. Don't be afraid to share your best practices or your failures. We learn faster when we learn together. There is no "stupid question" in a field this new.

Responsible Innovation

Use it powerfully, but safely. Don't be stifled by fear, but understand the boundaries. Innovation happens when we push limits within a safe framework.

No "Shadow AI"

Don't hide your tools. Using unapproved tools in secret compromises data security. If you find a great tool, bring it to the light so we can vet it and everyone can use it safely.

The "PhD Intern" Mindset

Treat the AI like a brilliant PhD-level intern who is new to the company. They are smart but lack context. They need guidance, clear instructions, and iteration to succeed.

Part 2: Context Engineering (The Unlock)

Prompt engineering is just the tip of the iceberg. Context Engineering is providing the background materials (PDFs, previous emails, data sets) so the AI actually "knows" what you know.

  • The Goldilocks Principle: Don't dump your entire hard drive. Don't give nothing. Give relevant documents that contain the answers you need.
  • Access to Tools: If you want it to analyze data, give it the spreadsheet. If you want it to summarize a meeting, give it the transcript.
  • Reference Examples: Show, don't just tell. "Write it like this [paste example]" is more powerful than describing the style for 5 paragraphs.

Troubleshooting & Navigation

Path Obsession

Sometimes the AI gets stuck in a loop, trying the same wrong solution over and over. Break the loop. Say "Stop. This isn't working. Let's try a completely different approach."

Context Transfer

Long chats get "stale" and the AI forgets early details. If a chat gets too long/confusing, summarize the key points and start a fresh chat with that summary as the new context.

Task Separation

Don't mix unrelated tasks in one thread. "New Task = New Chat." It keeps the context clean and prevents the AI from getting confused by previous instructions.

Assistants & Resources

Reusable tools, definitions, and libraries to accelerate your work.

The Vocabulary of Scale

Assistant

Helper for Tasks. An AI configured to help you with a specific, repetitive task (e.g., "Refine this email" or "Summarize this meeting"). You trigger it, you review it.

Automation

Workflow Helper. AI embedded into an existing process to remove manual steps. The process doesn't change, but the AI does the heavy lifting.

Agentic

Autonomous Execution. The AI is given a goal ("Plan a travel itinerary") and figures out the steps, executes them, and checks its own work. Often requires changing the process entirely.

Glossary of Terms

  • Hallucination: When an AI confidently states a fact that is incorrect or made up. Always verify critical facts.
  • Zero-shot: Asking the AI to do something without giving it any examples.
  • Few-shot: Giving the AI a few examples of "good" output to guide it. (Much more effective).
  • Context Window: The amount of information (text/tokens) the AI can "remember" in a single conversation.
  • Temperature: A setting that controls creativity. Low temp = precise/deterministic. High temp = creative/random.
