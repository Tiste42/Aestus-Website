Pool Question MT-020

Montana's administrative penalty on a LICENSEE for sale-to-minor violations is a progressive schedule over a rolling 3-year window. What does that structure mean in practice, and what can reduce a first offense?

  1. a. Every offense carries the same flat fine no matter the licensee's history
  2. b. Penalties escalate with each offense (fine, then a larger fine, then a fine plus license suspension, then revocation), and a first offense can be reduced to a reprimand if the licensee trained all staff Correct
  3. c. Only the fourth offense has a consequence; the first three are informal warnings
  4. d. The schedule resets to zero after each individual violation

ARM 42.13.101 sets a progressive schedule over a rolling 3-year window: each offense is penalized more severely than the last, up to license revocation. A documented staff-training program can reduce a first offense to a reprimand, so ongoing RASS training directly lowers the licensee's regulatory risk. The practical point is the escalation and the training incentive, not a specific dollar figure.

Pool Question MT-027

MCA 27-1-710 requires one of three specific triggers. Sober service that ends in a later, unrelated incident does not create dram shop exposure.

Pool Question MT-028

  3. c. $250,000 Correct
  4. d. Full $400,000 (no cap applies)

MCA 27-1-710(10): noneconomic damages are capped at $250,000 per dram shop event. Economic damages (medical, lost wages) are not capped.

Pool Question MT-029

Pool Question MT-030

An incident occurs on March 1, 2026. By what date must the injured party serve certified-mail notice on the licensee to preserve a Montana dram-shop claim?

  3. c. Within 90 days (by May 30)

MCA 27-1-710 requires the injured party to serve certified-mail notice WITHIN 180 days of the sale or service. Notice served after day 180 forfeits the dram-shop claim. The civil action itself must commence within 2 years of the sale or service.

  3. c. About 0.16 or higher. Into aggravated-DUI territory. Cut off and call a ride.

Three 12-oz beers at 7% ABV is approximately 4.2 standard drinks. For a 160 lb adult over one hour, BAC reaches roughly 0.07-0.08, right at the Montana per-se limit (MCA 61-8-1002). Don't pour a fourth; transition to water and food, and start a ride conversation.

A 220 lb customer at your Montana bar has had four 16-oz craft pints (about 8% ABV each) over 2 hours. Approximately what BAC has he reached and what is the right next move?

  1. a. About 0.04. Fine to keep serving. CDL territory only.
  2. b. About 0.10-0.13. Over the 0.08 per-se limit. Cut off, offer water and a comp ride.
  3. c. About 0.18. In aggravated-DUI territory at 0.16 or higher. Cut off, offer water and a comp ride, do not let him drive. Correct
  4. d. BAC depends only on the number of drinks, not the size or ABV.

Four 16-oz craft pints at 8% ABV is approximately 8.5 standard drinks over 2 hours. For a 220 lb adult, BAC reaches roughly 0.16-0.18, at or above the aggravated-DUI threshold (MCA 61-8-1001) and well past the 0.08 per-se limit (MCA 61-8-1002). Tolerance does not change the BAC math. Cut off and call a ride.
