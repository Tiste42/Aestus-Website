Montana Alcohol Server Training: What to Expect

The media reinforces the lesson. The full module content below is the required training material.

Lesson

Welcome to CertReady's Montana Responsible Alcohol Sales and Service training, the on-premises track. Over the next two hours you will learn the practical knowledge and the Montana-specific law you need to serve, mix, or supervise alcohol service legally and safely under Montana's Responsible Alcohol Sales, Service, and Delivery Act.

Note

About 'RASS'. You'll see this acronym throughout the course and on Montana's verification database. RASS stands for Responsible Alcohol Sales and Service. As of January 1, 2026, the Act is officially named the Responsible Alcohol Sales, Service, and Delivery Act (HB 211 added 'Delivery' to cover third-party drivers), but the industry still calls the program RASS.

When we say 'Montana RASS,' that's what we mean.

Law

MCA 16-4-1005 (summary): Montana law requires every licensee to ensure that each employee authorized to sell, serve, or deliver alcohol, plus each immediate supervisor, completes a state-approved training program within 60 days of the employee's date of hire and every 3 years thereafter. The statute's operative subsection (1)(a) requires "successful completion of training"; the timing requirements are set out elsewhere in the same section.

Lesson

Effective January 1, 2026, Montana law requires two distinct training tracks: an on-premises course of at least two hours (this course), and a separate off-premises course of at least one hour. The two tracks share a lot of universal content but the on-premises track goes deeper on intervention, dram shop, and table-side cut-off scenarios because that is where most over-service incidents happen.

Note

Why this matters now. HB 157 (2025), HB 196 (2025), and HB 211 (2025) changed Montana's alcohol-server framework on January 1, 2026. The on/off split is new. Digital ID coverage is new.

And third-party delivery now has its own license and training rules. Even servers who took an older RASS course need refreshed training to be compliant.

Learning List

Course modules

  • Module 2 — Alcohol and the body. BAC, absorption, standard drinks, what counts as alcohol under Montana law.
  • Module 3 — Recognizing intoxication and refusing service. The Montana 'actually, apparently, or obviously intoxicated' standard, behavioral and physical cues, refusal scripts.
  • Module 4 — Checking IDs. Montana's acceptable ID list including digital IDs (new in 2025) and tribal IDs, fake-ID detection, calculating the legal date of birth.
  • Module 5 — Sales to minors. Progressive penalty schedule, the narrow MCA 16-6-305(1)(a) exception (parent/guardian/medical/religious), common minor tactics and how to refuse them.
  • Module 6 — Dram shop and the three liability types. When a server can be sued, how Montana's three-trigger framework works, the damage caps that apply.
  • Module 7 — Hours, promotions, and Montana license categories. When you can sell, what promotions are allowed, how license types differ.
  • Module 8 — Montana DUI law and the server's role. Per-se BAC limits, implied consent, what to do when a customer wants to drive home.
Lesson

After all modules are complete and you have spent at least 120 minutes on content, the final exam will unlock. The exam is 40 multiple-choice questions drawn from a 64-question pool. You need 80% to pass under ARM 42.13.907 (adopted March 7, 2026).

If you score 70-79% on your first attempt, you may retake the final exam only. If you score below 70%, you must retake the entire program before another final-exam attempt.

Lesson

When you pass the final exam, CertReady issues you a certificate immediately. Your CertReady certificate is your Montana training credential. There is no separate state-issued license to chase.

Save the PDF, send a copy to your employer, and keep your certificate number handy in case anyone needs to verify it.

Note

Verification. Anyone (your employer, an auditor, the regulator) can verify your training at svc.mt.gov/dor/liquorserver/public/certsearch.aspx using your last name and date of birth. Per ARM 42.13.904 (as adopted March 7, 2026), CertReady submits training records electronically to Montana CARD within two weeks of your completion via the CARD-approved secure electronic process.

Note

Did your employer sign you up? If your bar, restaurant, or restaurant group is using CertReady, they have a business dashboard at certready.org/business. They can see your training progress in real time, get a notification when you pass, and see your expiration date so they can remind you when it's time to renew. You don't need to do anything for this.

If they enrolled you with their CertReady code, the link is already set up. You'll still get your own copy of the certificate by email.

Lesson

Your certificate is valid for 3 years from the completion date. About 60 days before it expires, CertReady will email you a reminder so you can re-take and stay compliant.
